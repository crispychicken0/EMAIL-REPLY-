Legal References

UAE Consumer Protection Law
Federal Law

The Federal Law No. (24) of 2008 on Consumer Protection provides guidelines for handling consumer complaints and disputes. According to Article 5, businesses must provide accurate information about products and services and resolve consumer complaints in a timely manner.

Key provisions relevant to compensation disputes:

  • Businesses must have clear complaint resolution procedures
  • Consumers have the right to request compensation for defective services
  • Businesses can dispute claims if they can prove the service was delivered as agreed
Platform Terms of Service
Contractual

Most delivery platforms include terms of service that outline their compensation policies. These typically specify:

  • Circumstances under which compensation is required
  • Documentation required to dispute a claim
  • Timeframes for responding to claims
  • Arbitration processes for unresolved disputes

It's important to review the specific terms for each platform you use.

Evidence Requirements
Best Practices

When disputing compensation claims, strong evidence is crucial. Recommended documentation includes:

  • Delivery confirmation with customer signature
  • GPS tracking data showing delivery to the correct address
  • Photos of the delivered order
  • Communication records with the customer
  • Time-stamped proof of delivery

Maintaining a systematic approach to collecting and storing this evidence can significantly improve your success rate in dispute resolution.
